Complete Guide

How to keep your plants thriving.

A care guide built for Sri Lanka's tropical climate. First-time plant parent or seasoned green thumb — this is everything you need.

🌿
The Basics

Six things every plant needs

01
💧

Watering

Most tropical plants prefer consistently moist soil but not waterlogged. Water when the top 2cm of soil feels dry. In Sri Lanka's heat, this usually means every 2–3 days. Reduce watering during the monsoon season.

02
☀️

Sunlight

Bright indirect light is ideal for most indoor plants. Place near a window with a sheer curtain. Avoid the harsh midday sun (10am–2pm), which can scorch leaves. Morning sun is perfectly fine.

03
🌱

Soil & Potting

Use well-draining soil mixed with coir dust and compost. All our decorated pots have drainage holes to prevent root rot. Repot every 12–18 months when roots start circling the pot.

04
🌡️

Temperature

Sri Lanka's tropical climate (24–32°C) is ideal for most plants we sell. Avoid placing plants near air conditioners or in draughty corridors. Most plants love our natural humidity.

05
🪴

Fertilising

Feed your plants every 2–4 weeks during the growing season (March–September) with a balanced liquid fertiliser diluted to half strength. Reduce feeding in cooler months.

06
🐛

Pest Control

Common pests include mealybugs, aphids and spider mites. Wipe leaves with neem oil solution weekly as prevention. For infestations, isolate the plant and treat with an insecticidal soap.

Plant-by-Plant

Care tips for your collection

Caring for Anthuriums

  • Light: Bright indirect light. They can tolerate lower light but will produce fewer flowers.
  • Water: Water when the top 2–3cm of soil is dry. They like consistent moisture but will rot if overwatered.
  • Humidity: Love humidity above 60% — perfect for most Sri Lankan homes. Mist occasionally during dry spells.
  • Feed: Use a phosphorus-rich fertiliser every 6 weeks for more blooms.
  • Common issue: Yellow leaves usually mean overwatering. Brown leaf tips mean low humidity or too much direct sun.

Caring for Orchids

  • Light: Bright indirect light. East-facing windows are ideal. Never place in direct afternoon sun.
  • Water: Water once a week by soaking the roots for 10 minutes, then let drain completely. Never let water sit in the pot.
  • Medium: Orchids don't use regular soil — they need bark chips or sphagnum moss for air circulation around roots.
  • Feed: Use orchid-specific fertiliser every 2 weeks during growing season. "Weakly, weekly" is the golden rule.
  • Blooming: After flowers drop, cut the stem above the second node. New flower spikes appear in 2–3 months.

Caring for Succulents & Cacti

  • Light: Full sun to bright light. These are the few plants that love Sri Lanka's direct sunlight.
  • Water: The "soak and dry" method — water thoroughly, then wait until soil is completely dry before watering again (every 7–10 days).
  • Soil: Must have excellent drainage. Mix regular soil with sand and perlite (50/50 ratio).
  • Monsoon caution: Move succulents under cover during heavy rains. They are very sensitive to overwatering and root rot.
  • Common issue: Mushy stems mean rot from overwatering. Remove affected parts immediately.

Caring for Ferns

  • Light: Indirect or filtered light. Ferns are understorey plants — they love shade and dappled light.
  • Water: Keep soil consistently moist. Ferns hate drying out. Water every 1–2 days in hot weather.
  • Humidity: The higher the better. Place on a pebble tray with water, or group with other plants. Bathrooms are ideal.
  • Feed: Diluted liquid fertiliser once a month during growing season. Too much fertiliser burns delicate fronds.
  • Pruning: Remove brown or dead fronds at the base to encourage new growth.
Seasonal Advice

🌧️ Monsoon Season Care (May–September)

Sri Lanka's south-west monsoon brings heavy rain and high humidity. Here's how to protect your plants:

Move plants away from windows

Avoid waterlogging from rain splash. Keep pots sheltered from direct monsoon winds.

Reduce watering frequency

The ambient humidity is doing half the work for you. Let the soil dry a bit more between waterings.

Watch for fungal issues

Increase air circulation around plants and avoid wetting leaves in the evening.

Perfect time for propagation!

The humidity makes it an ideal season to take cuttings and start new plants.
